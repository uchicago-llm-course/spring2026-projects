# 0. Finalized Distribution

## (1) “major queries”: 3 interv + 6 cf = 9 altogether

- **Definition**: (1) Causal Queries ; (2) w/ numerical answer format (which works much better with RLVR than binary)
    - **Intervention**: ATE, CATE, JATE
    - **Counterfactual**: CR, ATT, NIE, NDE, PN, PS

## (2) “minor queries”: 6 assoc + 3 interv + 1 cf = 10 altogether

- All non-major queries are minor queries. Specifically, they can be further classfied into:
    - **Association-Numerical**: marginal prob, conditional prob, joint prob, observed difference
    - **Association-Binary**: independence test*, Markov blanket*
    - **Intervention-Binary**: Identifiability*, Frontdoor adjustment*, Backdoor adjustment*
    - **Counterfactual-Binary**: Counterfactual fairness*

## (1.0) Results and Overarching Goals

- **The fundamental goals of domain translation**:
    1. Try to **hide the causal nature of the problem.** This way, a model trained on such data learns to recognize and exploit latent causal structure even when it is not explicitly labeled as causal, **thus simulating the real-world scenario to which we want our model to generalize** (e.g., clinical diagnosis, legal reasoning, etc.)
    2. At the same time, the translation must **preserve causal semantics exactly**: every diversified variant must produce the same final numerical answer as the original symbolic question.
- **TLDR**:
    1. Models’ performance on these domain-translated datasets, compared to the original symbolic datasets, does confirm the significance of our goals above: with exactly the same causal structures (DAG + SCMs)

## (1.1) Code

- **Design Variants:**
    1. **(same as before) To what degree do we specify an SCM?**
        1. **`dp`**: decimal places: 1-4
        2. **`condition_variants`** : how many SCMs to keep in the question: **full** & **surgery_ancestor_pruned**
    2. **(New): Structural variants in a piece of code:**
        1. **`code_structure`** : **single**-function VS **multi**-function;
            - note that `code_structure` is coupled with `condition_variants`: full → single-function, and surgery_ancestor_pruned → multi-function.
            - this coupling design is enforced due to the “topological data flow nature” of python code: it has to be read from top to bottom, so it’s impossible to place “the full causal graph but with pruned, incomplete CPTs” inside a single function.
        2. `control_flow_variants` (**`cf_variants`**): how to **represent the if-else branching logic in different ways**, possibly without if-else/match-case branches?
        3. `sampling_idom_variants` (**`si_variants`**): something not that important, but might be helpful in promoting diversity — how **the node value sampling process can be expressed in different ways**?
        4. `variable_naming_variants` (**`vn_variants`**): what if all the variables in the code snippet are named without any semantics (e.g., `var_{i}`), versus standard naming under probabilistic graph semantics (e.g., `cpd_{i}` , `p_{i}` , etc.)?
    3. **(different from before) How to verbalize the final question for different query types (`qv_variants`):**
        1. the design of this variant still pursues the fundamental goal of “hiding the causal nature of the problem” by formulating the question more like a **code reasoning** instead of an **explicitly causal reasoning** one.

## (1.2) Natural Language (NL)

- We adopted a two-step pipeline to convert a symbolic-form causal question into natural language, while adhering to the overarching goal of hiding its causal nature, so as to promote model’s generalization under real-world scenarios.

### Reference-guided entity assignment

- We constructed a corpus with 6K passages (length \in [100, 500] tokens) from three sources. Then, randomly sample a passage and feed it into `gpt-5.4-mini (thinking: low)` along with the original symbolic question, and instruct the model to assign a real-world entity to each node in the causal graph in a consistent way with the commensense.
    - **news (BBC-2025)**: https://huggingface.co/datasets/RealTimeData/bbc_news_alltime/viewer/2025-06
    - **wiki (Wikipedia-english-2024)**: https://huggingface.co/datasets/agentlans/wikipedia-paragraphs
    - **narrative (NarrSum-2022)**: https://github.com/zhaochaocs/narrasum
    
    | **news** | **wiki** | **narrative** |
    | --- | --- | --- |
    | 3000 | 2356 | 644 |
